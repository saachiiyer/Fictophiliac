WHO AM I?

My name is Saachi Iyer, and I'm 25 years old. I live here in Nagpur. While I currently work in an IT company, my real passions lie in the world of creativity. You could often find me lost in the pages of a good book, letting my imagination wander through different worlds and perspectives. This love for stories and language naturally spills over into my hobby of writing poems. For me, poetry is a way to capture fleeting emotions, to paint pictures with words, and to explore the nuances of life in a way that feels deeply personal. Beyond the literary, I also find immense joy in visual arts. Lippart Art, with its intricate details and the almost meditative process of filling in those beautiful patterns, really captivates me. There's something so satisfying about transforming a simple surface into something visually stunning, one careful dot and swirl at a time. Then there's canvas painting, which offers a broader canvas – literally! – for my creative ideas. I love the feel of the brush in my hand, the way colors blend and interact, and the freedom to bring the images in my mind to life. Whether it's bold and vibrant or soft and subtle, each painting feels like a piece of my inner world made tangible. Now, you might be wondering why someone with a seemingly stable job in IT is looking to start a webpage.

Well, honestly, lately, I've been feeling a bit… bored with the routine. While I appreciate the intellectual challenges of my work, there's a part of me, the part that loves to read, write, and create art, that feels a little unfulfilled. My hobbies aren't just things I do in my spare time; they're essential to who I am. They nourish a part of my soul that my current job doesn't quite reach. So, the idea of starting a webpage feels like a natural step for me. I envision it as a digital space where I can share my Lippart Art and Canvas paintings, almost like an online gallery where people can see what I've been creating. It would also be a place where I can share my poems, letting my verses find their way to those who might appreciate them. Maybe it could even evolve into a blog where I can talk about the books I'm reading, the inspirations behind my artwork, or even just my experiences trying to balance the logical world of IT with my more artistic inclinations. For me, this isn't just about putting my work online; it's about connecting my inner world with the outer. It's about creating a space where I can fully express the different facets of who I am, beyond just being an IT professional. I'm hoping this webpage will be a way to connect with others who appreciate art and creativity, and perhaps even a little spark to bring some new excitement and purpose into my life, offering a vibrant contrast to the everyday routine. It feels like an exciting step towards integrating the logical and the creative sides of myself in a more meaningful way.

What are different types of Genres that you can explore?

What is a Genre?

A genre is essentially a category used to classify artistic works, such as literature, music, film, and other forms of art and entertainment, based on shared conventions, styles, themes, or techniques. Think of it as a way of grouping things that have similar characteristics.  

Here's a breakdown of what that means:

Shared Conventions: Genres often have established rules, tropes, or elements that audiences and creators recognize. For example, a mystery novel typically involves a crime, clues, suspects, and a detective trying to solve it.   Style: The way an artwork is executed can define its genre. For instance, the fast-paced editing and intense action sequences are hallmarks of the action movie genre. Themes: Certain subjects or underlying ideas are common in specific genres. Science fiction often explores themes of technology, the future, or societal change.   Techniques: The methods used to create the artwork can also contribute to its genre classification. For example, the use of rhyme and meter is a common technique in poetry.   Genres help creators understand audience expectations and provide a framework for their work. They also help audiences find the kind of art they enjoy. It's important to remember that genres aren't always rigid, and many works can blend elements from multiple genres, creating interesting and innovative results!   Sources and related content

    HOLLY JACKSON

    Holly Jackson, born in 1992 in Buckinghamshire, England, is a British author known for her young adult mystery novels. She began writing stories at a young age and completed her first novel attempt at fifteen. Jackson holds a master's degree in English with a focus on literary linguistics and creative writing from the University of Nottingham. She is best known for her debut novel, A Good Girl's Guide to Murder (2019), a young adult mystery thriller that became an international bestseller. The book follows a high school student who reopens a closed murder case. This was followed by sequels Good Girl, Bad Blood (2020) and As Good As Dead (2021), as well as a prequel novella, Kill Joy (2021), completing the A Good Girl's Guide to Murder series. Beyond this series, Jackson has also written standalone novels including Five Survive (2022) and The Reappearance of Rachel Price (2024). Her writing often involves intricate plots and suspenseful narratives. She currently lives in London and enjoys playing video games and watching true crime documentaries.

    TAYLOR JENKINS REID

    Taylor Jenkins Reid (born December 20, 1983) is a prominent American author known for her compelling novels that often explore themes of love, fame, and the complexities of human relationships. She gained widespread recognition for her bestselling books such as The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo, Daisy Jones & The Six (which was adapted into a successful miniseries), Malibu Rising, and Carrie Soto Is Back. Reid's writing is characterized by engaging narratives, well-developed characters, and a captivating storytelling style that has garnered her a large and dedicated readership. She lives in Los Angeles with her husband and daughter.

    ALI HAZELWOOD

    Ali Hazelwood is an Italian author and former neuroscience professor known for her popular contemporary romance novels, often featuring women in STEM fields and academia. Originally from Italy, she lived in Germany and Japan before moving to the U.S. to pursue her Ph.D. in neuroscience. Her debut novel, The Love Hypothesis (2021), was a New York Times bestseller and gained significant popularity on TikTok. She has since written several other successful romance novels, including Love on the Brain (2022), Love, Theoretically (2023), Bride (2024), and Not in Love (2024), as well as novellas compiled in Loathe to Love You (2023) and Deep End (2025). Her works are characterized by their humor, focus on smart and driven female characters, and engaging romantic relationships, often utilizing tropes like fake dating and enemies-to-lovers.
